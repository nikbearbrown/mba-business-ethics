# Figure briefs — Chapter 7: What Employees Owe Employers

## Figure 7.1: The two columns of duty

**Placement:** Chapter opening, after the Watkins cold open.

**Description:** Two-column ledger. Left: "What employee owes employer" (loyalty, confidentiality, performance, brand respect, conduct, financial integrity). Right: "What employee owes parties beyond the firm" (honesty in regulatory filings, non-participation in fraud, disclosure under whistleblower statutes, public/shareholders harmed by ongoing wrongdoing).

**Pedagogical purpose:** The chapter's question is what to do when the two columns disagree.

**Style:** Plain ledger-style table, serif.

## Figure 7.2: The four restrictive covenants

**Placement:** Concept 1, after the four-clause introduction.

**Description:** Four-row table with each clause: Non-Compete (purpose / typical duration / state-variation note), NDA (purpose / scope / enforceability), Nonsolicitation (purpose / scope / enforceability), Nondisparagement (purpose / contestation note).

**Style:** Plain table, serif, no color.

## Figure 7.3: The four types of workplace violence

**Placement:** Concept 2, after the workplace-violence subsection.

**Description:** Four-quadrant grid. Type 1 (criminal intent): robbery; Type 2 (customer-on-worker, ~20% homicides); Type 3 (worker-on-worker, ~15% homicides); Type 4 (personal-relationship-into-workplace, ~10%, women disproportionately affected). Each quadrant lists structural prevention.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Single uniform policy will be inadequate to all four types; prevention must be type-specific.

**Style:** 2x2 grid, plain serif.

---

## Figure 7.4: The whistleblower decision tree

**Placement:** Concept 3, after the breaking-point introduction.

**Description:** Decision-tree flowchart. Start: "Employee discovers misconduct." Branch 1: Is internal channel uncompromised? → If yes: internal report → If response inadequate: external. Branch 2: Is external channel needed (internal compromised)? → identify applicable statute (FCA, SOX, IRS Whistleblower, Dodd-Frank, OSHA). Branch 3: Career-consequence assessment before disclosure. End: documented record + chosen action.

**Pedagogical purpose:** The whistleblower decision is procedural; the procedure protects both the disclosure and the discloser.

**Style:** Flowchart, plain serif, no color.
