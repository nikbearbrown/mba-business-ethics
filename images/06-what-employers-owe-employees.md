# Figure briefs — Chapter 6: What Employers Owe Employees

## Figure 6.1: Legal floor / Operative culture

**Placement:** Chapter opening, after Sharon Dunn cold open.

**Description:** Two-column table. Left: "What the law requires" (Civil Rights Act 1964, OSHA 1970, Equal Pay Act 1963, WARN 1989, EEOC complaints, Title VII). Right: "What ethical employer also owes" (operative culture, enforcement when costly, real wage growth, dignity in monitoring, voice in governance, accommodation of human life outside work).

**Pedagogical purpose:** Right column doesn't contradict left; operates in space left silent.

**Style:** Plain table, serif, no color.

## Figure 6.2: The four legal pillars

**Placement:** Concept 1, after the four-statute introduction.

**Description:** Four-column table — OSHA / Title VII-EEOC / WARN / ECPA. Rows: what it prohibits, what it requires, max penalty, most-cited case.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Pillars cover safety, discrimination, layoff notice, privacy. None covers wage adequacy or operative culture (left to ethics).

**Style:** Plain table.

## Figure 6.3: The four pay gaps

**Placement:** Concept 2, after the gap-mechanism introduction.

**Description:** Four-panel comparison: (1) U.S. real minimum wage 1968-2024 (declining); (2) U.S. productivity vs. wages 1973-2016 (productivity +74%, wages +12%); (3) CEO-to-worker pay ratio 1965-2014 (20:1 → 300:1); (4) Gender pay gap 1960-2024 (60% → 82%). EPI and BLS data sources noted.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Four gaps tell related parts of the same post-1973 decoupling story.

**Style:** Four-panel grid, plain serif, no color, axes labeled.

## Figure 6.4: The autonomy triad — privacy, voice, due process

**Placement:** Concept 3, after the three claims introduction.

**Description:** Three-circle diagram. Each circle labeled with one autonomy claim. Inside each, U.S. legal protection level rated against OECD median: Privacy (low, ECPA permissive), Voice (low, ~10% unionization), Due Process (low, employment-at-will).

**Pedagogical purpose:** U.S. operates at OECD low end on all three dimensions.

**Style:** Three circles, plain serif, no color.
