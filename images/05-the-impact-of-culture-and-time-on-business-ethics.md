# Figure briefs — Chapter 5: The Impact of Culture and Time on Business Ethics

## Figure 5.1: 1626 / 2026 lower Manhattan split panel

**Placement:** Chapter opening, after the Manhattan cold-open scene.

**Description:** Side-by-side panels. Left: 1626 Manhattan, sketched simply — Lenape settlement, Dutch ship at anchor, sparse forest. Right: 2026 lower Manhattan, financial district skyline. Single horizontal line connects the two panels with the label "the same place, four hundred years apart."

**Pedagogical purpose:** Student should notice that the *place* is constant; the cultural-economic framework determining what counts as ethical business is not. The figure makes the chapter's central premise visual.

**Style notes:** Two simple line-art panels, plain serif labels. No color. Connecting line between the panels rendered as a thin horizontal rule.

## Figure 5.2: Explicit values vs. operative culture

**Placement:** Concept 1, after the IBM / Merck stated-beliefs paragraph.

**Description:** Two-column table.

| What the firm states it values | What the firm's behavior values |
|---|---|
| Mission statement | Compensation structures |
| Code of conduct | Who gets promoted |
| Employee handbook | What is rewarded under pressure |
| Public commitments | What is punished |
| Annual reports | What is funded vs. starved |

**Pedagogical purpose:** Student should notice that for most firms, the right column is the operative culture and that the left column is, at best, an aspiration. The diagnostic is to read both and identify the gap.

**Style notes:** Plain table, serif. No color.

## Figure 5.3: Five historical eras of business ethics

**Placement:** Concept 2, after the introduction of the five-era framework.

**Description:** Horizontal timeline running from 1500 to 2030, with the five eras as labeled bands. For each era, one defining case study is anchored in the band:

- Mercantilist (1500-1750): Manhattan transaction 1626
- Industrial (1750-1920): Dickensian factory ~1840
- Postindustrial (1920-1970): Bernays Lucky Strike "torches of freedom" 1929
- Information (1970-2010): Cambridge Analytica 2018 / Equifax breaches 2010s
- Globalization and after (2010-present): the contested present

**Pedagogical purpose:** Student should see that ethical questions are era-specific — what was contested in one era was settled in the next, and what was unimagined in one era became central in the next.

**Style notes:** Horizontal timeline, era bands shaded differently (different hatch patterns, no color). Case-study anchors as small labeled markers.

## Figure 5.4: The thin universal core and the thick local variation

**Placement:** Concept 4, after the discussion of what survives translation.

**Description:** Venn diagram with two large overlapping circles.

- Left circle: "Thin universal core" — items inside: honesty, non-coercion, fair dealing, stewardship, persons-as-ends.
- Right circle: "Thick local norms" — items inside: time orientation, contact distance, gift-giving practice, religious accommodation, dress code, written-vs-oral contract preference.
- Center overlap labeled "operationalization" — the place where the universal core is enacted through the local norms.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Student should notice that the thin universal core is operationalized through the thick local norms and that neither alone gives the firm what it needs. The overlap is the operational reality of cross-cultural ethics.

**Style notes:** Two overlapping circles, plain serif. No color hierarchy. The overlap region visually distinct (lighter hatch) to mark its importance.
