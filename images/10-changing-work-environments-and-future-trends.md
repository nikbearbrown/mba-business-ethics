# Figure briefs — Chapter 10: Changing Work Environments and Future Trends

## Figure 10.1: Workplace transitions timeline 1970-2035

**Placement:** Chapter opening, after McDonald's scene.

**Description:** Horizontal timeline showing overlapping waves: telecommuting (1970s coined → widespread post-2010), gig economy (2010s, ~35% workforce by 2018), automation waves (cashier kiosks 2010s, AVs 2020s, AI knowledge work 2020s+), workplace campus consolidation (Apple Park 2017, Willow Village 2020s).

**Pedagogical purpose:** Transitions overlap and accelerate; no transition finishes before next begins.

**Style:** Plain timeline, serif.

## Figure 10.2: Productivity vs. cultural costs of remote work

**Placement:** Concept 1, after the Bloom data.

**Description:** Two-column comparison. Left: documented productivity gains (Bloom 13.5%/50% retention; JD Edwards 20-25%; AmEx 43%). Right: documented cultural costs (visibility bias, weakened collaboration, blurred work-life). Each side with citations.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Both sides of remote-work debate are real; framework is to capture gains while mitigating costs.

**Style:** Two-column table, plain serif.

## Figure 10.3: McKinsey 2017 automation projections

**Placement:** Concept 2, after the McKinsey introduction.

**Description:** Four-panel data display. Panel 1: global 800M / 20% of workforce. Panel 2: U.S. 39-73M / 25-50% of workforce. Panel 3: high-risk vs. low-risk occupation lists. Panel 4: U.S. vs. India displacement comparison (showing labor-cost effect).

**Pedagogical purpose:** Aggregate numbers obscure sector and country specifics that determine where displacement actually lands.

**Style:** Four-panel grid, plain serif, no color.

## Figure 10.4: AI workplace ethics — the three open questions

**Placement:** Concept 3, after the disclosure/liability/personhood introduction.

**Description:** Three-column table. Column 1 (Disclosure): California B.O.T. Act 2018, EU AI Act 2024, U.S. national framework in development. Column 2 (Liability): EU tiered framework, U.S. case-by-case. Column 3 (Personhood): unresolved philosophically and legally.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Three questions sit at different states of resolution; firms must make operational decisions on all three regardless.

**Style:** Three-column table, plain serif, no color.
