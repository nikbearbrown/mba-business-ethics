# Figure briefs — Chapter 8: Recognizing and Respecting the Rights of All

## Figure 8.1: Workplace-rights statutes timeline 1964-2024

**Placement:** Chapter opening, after Aimee Stephens scene.

**Description:** Horizontal timeline. Anchors: Civil Rights Act 1964 (Title VII), Equal Pay Act 1963, Age Discrimination Act 1967, Pregnancy Discrimination Act 1978, ADA 1990, FMLA 1993, Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act 2009, ADA Amendments 2008, Bostock 2020.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Framework built incrementally over 60 years; substantial gaps between 1960s-70s framing era and recent consolidation.

**Style:** Horizontal timeline, plain serif, no color.

## Figure 8.2: U.S. workforce demographics — three panels

**Placement:** Concept 1, after the data anchors.

**Description:** Three panels. Panel 1: Female labor-force participation 1977 (48%) → 2017 (57%) → projected 2050 (~60%). Panel 2: Tech sector vs. all private sector (white %, Asian %, women %). Panel 3: Google 2017 published demographics by overall / technical roles / leadership.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Aggregate numbers obscure where underrepresentation lives — in technical roles and leadership.

**Style:** Three side-by-side bar/line charts, plain serif, no color hierarchy.

## Figure 8.3: The reasonable-accommodation framework

**Placement:** Concept 2, after the mechanism introduction.

**Description:** Decision flowchart. Start: "Employee has condition (disability, religious belief, similar deeply-held commitment)." Branch 1: Is accommodation requested or evident? → Engage in interactive process. Branch 2: Is the accommodation reasonable (does not eliminate essential functions)? Branch 3: Does it impose undue hardship (significant difficulty/expense relative to firm size)? End: provide accommodation OR document undue hardship.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Accommodation is a procedural framework, not a binary yes/no.

**Style:** Flowchart, plain serif, no color.

---

## Figure 8.4: Income share at the top vs. middle-class decline

**Placement:** Concept 3, after data anchors.

**Description:** Two superimposed line graphs over 1970-2024. Line 1: top 1% pre-tax income share (rises from ~8% to ~22%). Line 2: middle-income household share (declines from ~60% to ~47%). The two lines tell parallel parts of the same story.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Income concentration at the top and middle-class contraction are not separate phenomena.

**Style:** Two-line graph, plain serif, axes clearly labeled.
